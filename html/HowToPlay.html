About the Game

The world of entrepreneurship is large, full of possibility, and--most of all-- random. The best method to teach you of the process behind and entrepreneurial venture is, and always will be, experience.

In this simulation, you take the role of an entrepreneur who is in the process of starting their own business. In order to begin your process, though, you must have the courage to pursue and market a new idea or invention to the public.

You will also need money. Fortunately, you start with enough to begin the pursuit of one venture, but you can stay at your day job for longer in order to acquire more starting funds.

As you go through your venture, you will fail. A lot. But after failure comes success.

Getting Started

In this game you have a choice of playing against other people or against the computer. You can play up to six players, so you first need to select your competition. When you select your competition you need to select a name and color of the business.Make sure you did the same for your own business and now you are ready to get started.

Basic Rules

The main objective of this game is to obtain more money than all the other players. Each player will start with an equal amount of money to help them begin. The idea is to create products and take them through the process an entrepreneur would go through. There are nine different phases a product will go through idea, design, prototype, testing prototype, development, testing prerelease product, deploy, testing post-release product, and maintenance. During a round a player will have a choice to do nothing, create a product, add employees, or move to the next product phase. Once you end your turn you will either gain money or lose money. Next the other player will have to do the same thing during their turn and so on until the final round is over

Additional Rules

At the beginning of the game you can choice to do nothing each round and you will gain a set amount of money each round. This can be very beneficial at first since you might not have enough money to create your first product. Once your product is created you can hire different people to help you at each phase. There is a cost for each person you hire, so might want to be careful not to hire to many people. The people you hire will help you during certain phases of your product. Throughout the game there will be random events that can either help you or make things more difficult. For example you can get a tax break and increase your fund. Or the power goes out in your building and you need to send workers home.
